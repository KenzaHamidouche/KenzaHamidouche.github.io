About me

    I am a post-doctoral researcher at ORFE, Princeton University working on multi-agent reinforcement learning with Prof. Warren Powell, as part of a project with Neutigers. Prior to that, I obtained a PhD in telecommunication from CentraleSupélec under the supervision of Prof. Mérouane Debbah and Prof. Walid Saad. During my PhD, I worked on the application of game theory and machine learning for resource allocation in 5G networks. I received bachelor's and master's degrees in computer science from the Ecole Nationale Supérieure d'Informatique and the University of Rennes 1, respectively. In 2015, I was a visiting scholar at Virginia Tech.

    My research interests are in the broad areas of game theory, distributed learning, mechanism design, learning under uncertainty and reinforcement learning. Applications of particular interest include wireless communications, Internet of things, pricing, autonomous multi-agent systems, security and privacy.
